# Staff Procedures and Protocols

## Reception Staff Procedures

### Patient Check-In Process

**Step 1: Greet the Patient**
- Welcome patient warmly and professionally
- Ask for patient name and appointment time
- Verify patient identity with photo ID

**Step 2: Verify Information**
- Confirm appointment in the system
- Check if patient information is current
- Update any changes to address, phone, insurance
- Scan insurance card if updated

**Step 3: Collect Payment**
- Review insurance coverage and co-pay amount
- Collect co-payment before appointment
- Process payment in billing system
- Provide payment receipt

**Step 4: Complete Check-In**
- Mark patient as "Checked-In" in the system
- Provide any necessary forms to complete
- Direct patient to waiting area
- Notify medical staff of patient arrival
- Estimated wait time: 5-10 minutes

### New Patient Registration

**Required Information:**
- Full legal name (as appears on ID)
- Date of birth
- Current address and contact information
- Emergency contact name and phone number
- Insurance information (if applicable)
- Primary care physician (if transferring)
- Known allergies and current medications
- Brief medical history

**Registration Steps:**
1. Create new patient profile in system
2. Scan patient ID and insurance card
3. Have patient complete medical history form
4. Send email verification link
5. Collect registration fee if applicable
6. Provide patient portal access instructions
7. Schedule initial consultation appointment

### Appointment Scheduling

**Booking Guidelines:**
- Verify doctor availability before confirming
- General consultations: 30-minute slots
- Follow-ups: 20-minute slots
- Specialists: 45-minute slots
- New patient physicals: 1-hour slots

**Scheduling Best Practices:**
- Book urgent cases within 24-48 hours
- Routine appointments: 1-2 weeks out
- Preventive care: flexible scheduling
- Allow buffer time between complex cases
- Coordinate multiple appointments when possible

**Appointment Confirmations:**
- Confirm all appointments via phone or email
- Call 24 hours before appointment
- Send SMS reminder 2 hours before (if enabled)
- Document all confirmation attempts
- Flag no-shows in patient record

### Handling Phone Calls

**Incoming Call Protocol:**
1. Answer within 3 rings
2. Greet: "Good [morning/afternoon], [Clinic Name], this is [Your Name], how may I help you?"
3. Listen actively and take notes
4. Address inquiry or route to appropriate staff
5. Confirm next steps before ending call
6. Thank caller for contacting the clinic

**Call Types and Routing:**
- Appointment scheduling: Handle directly
- Prescription refills: Transfer to nurse line
- Billing questions: Transfer to billing department
- Medical emergencies: Transfer to nurse or advise to call 911
- Test results: Take message for doctor callback
- Technical issues: Transfer to IT support

## Doctor Procedures

### Daily Workflow

**Morning Routine (30 minutes before first appointment):**
- Log into system and review day's schedule
- Check messages and lab results
- Review patient charts for scheduled appointments
- Identify any urgent cases or follow-ups
- Brief meeting with nursing staff

**During Appointments:**
- Review patient history before entering room
- Spend full allocated time with each patient
- Document findings in real-time when possible
- Create clear, detailed notes
- Discuss treatment plans and next steps
- Answer patient questions thoroughly

**End of Day (30 minutes after last appointment):**
- Complete all patient notes and documentation
- Respond to patient messages
- Review pending lab results
- Schedule any needed follow-up appointments
- Sign off on prescriptions and referrals
- Update next day's schedule if needed

### Medical Documentation Standards

**Every Patient Note Must Include:**
- Chief complaint in patient's own words
- Relevant medical history
- Current medications and allergies
- Physical examination findings
- Assessment and diagnosis
- Treatment plan and recommendations
- Follow-up instructions
- Patient education provided
- Time spent with patient

**Documentation Timing:**
- Notes must be completed within 24 hours of appointment
- Same-day completion is preferred
- Hospital notes: within 12 hours
- Prescriptions: immediately after appointment
- Referrals: same business day

### Prescription Guidelines

**E-Prescription Requirements:**
- Verify patient allergies before prescribing
- Check for drug interactions
- Include clear dosage and duration
- Specify number of refills
- Add administration instructions
- Select patient's preferred pharmacy
- Controlled substances require extra verification

**Prescription Refills:**
- Review patient request within 24 hours
- Verify last appointment date
- Check if medication requires office visit
- Approve or schedule appointment for evaluation
- Document decision in patient chart

### Managing Test Results

**Receiving Results:**
- Review all incoming lab and imaging results daily
- Flag abnormal or critical values
- Compare with previous results when available
- Create action plan for abnormal findings

**Communicating Results:**
- Normal results: Posted to patient portal with brief note
- Abnormal results: Phone call to patient within 24 hours
- Critical results: Immediate phone call, document attempts
- Always document how results were communicated
- Schedule follow-up if treatment changes needed

## Clinic Administrator Procedures

### Daily Administrative Tasks

**Morning Checklist:**
- Review previous day's appointments and no-shows
- Check system for any technical issues
- Review staff schedules and coverage
- Monitor patient complaints or feedback
- Verify supply inventory levels

**Weekly Tasks:**
- Generate appointment and revenue reports
- Review and approve staff time-off requests
- Conduct staff meetings
- Process new patient registrations
- Update doctor schedules and availability
- Review billing and payment collections

**Monthly Tasks:**
- Analyze clinic performance metrics
- Review staff performance
- Update clinic policies if needed
- Conduct inventory and supply orders
- Generate financial reports for management
- Plan staff training and development

### Staff Management

**Onboarding New Staff:**
1. Complete HR paperwork and background checks
2. Create system login credentials
3. Assign appropriate role and permissions
4. Provide system training (minimum 2 days)
5. Shadow experienced staff for 1 week
6. Assign mentor for first 30 days
7. 30-day and 90-day performance reviews

**Performance Management:**
- Monthly one-on-one meetings with each staff member
- Address performance issues promptly
- Document all discussions and actions
- Provide ongoing training and development
- Recognize and reward excellent performance
- Maintain confidential personnel files

### Handling Patient Complaints

**Complaint Resolution Process:**
1. Listen to patient without interrupting
2. Express empathy and concern
3. Apologize for inconvenience or issue
4. Document complaint in detail
5. Investigate the situation thoroughly
6. Determine appropriate resolution
7. Follow up with patient within 48 hours
8. Implement changes to prevent recurrence
9. Log complaint in quality assurance system

**Common Complaint Types:**
- Long wait times: Review scheduling practices
- Billing errors: Involve billing department
- Staff behavior: Address in private with staff member
- Medical care concerns: Involve doctor and risk management
- System/technical issues: Contact IT support

### Emergency Response

**Medical Emergency in Clinic:**
1. Call 911 immediately if life-threatening
2. Alert all available medical staff
3. Begin first aid/CPR if trained and necessary
4. Clear area of other patients
5. Gather patient's medical information
6. Meet emergency responders at entrance
7. Document incident completely
8. Follow up with patient/family
9. File incident report within 24 hours

**System/Security Emergency:**
- System outage: Switch to backup paper procedures
- Fire: Evacuate per fire safety plan
- Security threat: Contact law enforcement and secure staff/patients
- Natural disaster: Follow emergency preparedness plan
- Data breach: Contact IT and legal immediately

## Quality Assurance

### Patient Safety Protocols
- Verify patient identity before all procedures
- Use two patient identifiers (name and DOB)
- Verify allergies before medication administration
- Document all adverse events immediately
- Report safety concerns to management
- Participate in monthly safety training

### Infection Control
- Hand hygiene before and after patient contact
- Use appropriate personal protective equipment
- Clean and disinfect exam rooms between patients
- Properly dispose of medical waste
- Follow isolation precautions when indicated
- Annual infection control training required

### Compliance Requirements
- HIPAA privacy training annually
- Maintain patient confidentiality at all times
- Secure all patient information
- Report privacy breaches immediately
- Follow documentation standards
- Participate in compliance audits
