# Medical Protocols and Clinical Guidelines

## General Medical Protocols

### Patient Vital Signs - Standard Procedures

**Temperature Measurement**
- Normal range: 97.8°F - 99.1°F (36.5°C - 37.3°C)
- Method: Oral, tympanic, or temporal artery
- Document method used
- Fever threshold: 100.4°F (38°C) or higher
- Take vital signs for all new patients and follow-ups

**Blood Pressure Measurement**
- Patient should be seated and relaxed for 5 minutes
- Arm at heart level, feet flat on floor
- Use appropriate cuff size
- Normal: Systolic <120 and Diastolic <80
- Elevated: Systolic 120-129 and Diastolic <80
- Hypertension Stage 1: Systolic 130-139 or Diastolic 80-89
- Hypertension Stage 2: Systolic ≥140 or Diastolic ≥90
- Hypertensive Crisis: Systolic >180 and/or Diastolic >120 (seek immediate care)

**Heart Rate**
- Normal resting: 60-100 beats per minute
- Athletes: 40-60 beats per minute
- Tachycardia: >100 bpm at rest
- Bradycardia: <60 bpm at rest
- Document rhythm irregularities

**Respiratory Rate**
- Normal adult: 12-20 breaths per minute
- Count for full 60 seconds
- Note any difficulty breathing
- Document oxygen saturation if available (normal: >95%)

**Weight and Height**
- Measure at every annual physical
- Calculate BMI (Body Mass Index)
- Track changes from previous visits
- BMI categories:
  - Underweight: <18.5
  - Normal: 18.5-24.9
  - Overweight: 25-29.9
  - Obese: ≥30

### Pain Assessment Protocol

**Pain Scale (0-10)**
- 0: No pain
- 1-3: Mild pain (minimal interference with activities)
- 4-6: Moderate pain (interferes with some activities)
- 7-9: Severe pain (unable to perform normal activities)
- 10: Worst pain imaginable

**Documentation Requirements**
- Location of pain
- Quality (sharp, dull, burning, throbbing)
- Duration and onset
- Aggravating and relieving factors
- Current pain management strategies
- Impact on daily activities and sleep
- Previous pain treatments and effectiveness

### Medication Administration Protocol

**Five Rights of Medication Administration**
1. Right Patient - Verify with two identifiers (name and DOB)
2. Right Medication - Check prescription against order
3. Right Dose - Verify amount and concentration
4. Right Route - Oral, topical, injection, etc.
5. Right Time - Administer at prescribed intervals

**Before Administration:**
- Review patient allergies
- Check for drug interactions
- Verify patient can swallow (for oral medications)
- Explain medication purpose to patient
- Document lot number and expiration date

**After Administration:**
- Document time, dose, and route
- Monitor for adverse reactions
- Document patient response
- Store medications properly
- Dispose of materials safely

### Infection Control Protocols

**Hand Hygiene**
- Wash hands before and after every patient contact
- Use alcohol-based sanitizer when hands not visibly soiled
- Wash for minimum 20 seconds with soap and water
- Hand washing required:
  - Before and after patient contact
  - Before clean/aseptic procedures
  - After body fluid exposure risk
  - After touching patient surroundings
  - Before eating or preparing food

**Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)**
- Gloves: For any contact with blood, body fluids, or contaminated items
- Masks: For respiratory symptoms or airborne precautions
- Gowns: For procedures with splash risk
- Eye protection: For splash or spray procedures
- Dispose of PPE immediately after use
- Never reuse disposable PPE

**Exam Room Sanitization**
- Clean all surfaces between patients
- Use EPA-approved disinfectants
- Pay special attention to high-touch surfaces:
  - Door handles
  - Light switches
  - Exam table
  - Blood pressure cuff
  - Stethoscope
  - Computer keyboard and mouse
- Allow proper contact time for disinfectant
- Use clean linens for each patient

## Common Clinical Procedures

### Blood Draw (Venipuncture) Protocol

**Preparation:**
- Verify order and required tests
- Gather supplies: tourniquet, needles, tubes, alcohol swabs, gauze, bandage
- Confirm patient fasting status if required
- Check patient allergies (latex, adhesive)
- Label tubes with patient information

**Procedure Steps:**
1. Verify patient identity with two identifiers
2. Explain procedure to patient
3. Position patient's arm comfortably
4. Apply tourniquet 3-4 inches above site
5. Select vein (antecubital fossa preferred)
6. Clean site with alcohol swab in circular motion
7. Allow site to air dry
8. Insert needle at 15-30 degree angle
9. Collect blood in appropriate tubes in correct order
10. Release tourniquet before removing needle
11. Apply pressure with gauze
12. Dispose of needle in sharps container
13. Label tubes and send to lab
14. Document procedure

**Post-Procedure Care:**
- Apply bandage
- Instruct patient to apply pressure for 3-5 minutes
- Advise patient to report excessive bleeding or bruising
- Document any complications

### Wound Care Protocol

**Assessment:**
- Measure wound dimensions (length, width, depth)
- Describe wound type: laceration, abrasion, puncture, burn
- Document location and surrounding tissue condition
- Check for foreign bodies
- Assess circulation and sensation
- Document tetanus immunization status

**Cleaning and Treatment:**
- Irrigate with sterile saline or clean water
- Remove debris and dead tissue if necessary
- Apply antibiotic ointment if indicated
- Cover with appropriate dressing
- Secure with tape or bandage
- Elevate if possible to reduce swelling

**Dressing Changes:**
- Change dressing daily or when wet/soiled
- Assess for signs of infection:
  - Increased redness or warmth
  - Swelling
  - Pus or drainage
  - Increased pain
  - Fever
- Document wound progress with each change

**Follow-Up Instructions:**
- Keep wound clean and dry
- Watch for signs of infection
- Return for suture removal if applicable (7-14 days)
- Complete full course of antibiotics if prescribed
- Return immediately if fever or increasing pain

### Immunization Administration

**Pre-Vaccination:**
- Review immunization history
- Check for contraindications and allergies
- Verify vaccine storage temperature
- Explain benefits and risks to patient
- Obtain consent

**Administration Sites:**
- Infants/toddlers: Anterolateral thigh
- Children/adults: Deltoid muscle
- Subcutaneous: Fatty tissue of upper arm or thigh

**Post-Vaccination:**
- Observe patient for 15 minutes for adverse reactions
- Provide vaccine information sheet
- Document vaccine name, lot number, expiration date, site, and dose
- Schedule next vaccination if series
- Advise on common side effects:
  - Soreness at injection site
  - Low-grade fever
  - Mild rash
- Provide emergency contact information

## Chronic Disease Management

### Diabetes Management Protocol

**Type 2 Diabetes Monitoring:**
- HbA1c every 3 months (target <7% for most patients)
- Fasting blood glucose: 80-130 mg/dL
- Random blood glucose: <180 mg/dL
- Annual foot exam
- Annual eye exam
- Annual kidney function tests
- Blood pressure monitoring at each visit (target <140/90)
- Lipid panel annually

**Patient Education:**
- Proper glucose monitoring technique
- Medication adherence
- Dietary guidelines (carb counting, portion control)
- Exercise recommendations (150 minutes/week)
- Foot care and daily inspection
- Sick day management
- Recognition of hypoglycemia and hyperglycemia

**Hypoglycemia Management:**
- Symptoms: shakiness, sweating, confusion, dizziness
- Treatment: 15g fast-acting carbohydrate
- Recheck glucose in 15 minutes
- Repeat if still low
- Follow with protein/complex carb snack
- Document episode and probable cause

### Hypertension Management Protocol

**Blood Pressure Targets:**
- General population: <130/80 mmHg
- Diabetes or kidney disease: <130/80 mmHg
- Age >65 years: <130/80 mmHg

**Monitoring Schedule:**
- Newly diagnosed: Every 2-4 weeks until controlled
- Stable: Every 3-6 months
- Medication changes: Follow-up in 2-4 weeks

**Lifestyle Modifications:**
- DASH diet (low sodium, high potassium)
- Sodium restriction: <2,300 mg/day (ideally <1,500 mg)
- Weight loss if overweight (target BMI <25)
- Regular aerobic exercise: 150 minutes/week
- Limit alcohol: ≤2 drinks/day for men, ≤1 for women
- Smoking cessation
- Stress management

**Medication Management:**
- Start with lifestyle modifications
- Add medication if BP remains elevated after 3 months
- Consider multiple agents if significantly elevated
- Monitor for side effects
- Check kidney function before and after starting ACE/ARB
- Annual lab work: electrolytes, creatinine, lipids

### Asthma Management Protocol

**Severity Classification:**
- Intermittent: Symptoms ≤2 days/week
- Mild Persistent: Symptoms >2 days/week but not daily
- Moderate Persistent: Daily symptoms
- Severe Persistent: Symptoms throughout the day

**Peak Flow Monitoring:**
- Measure daily for moderate-severe asthma
- Green Zone: 80-100% of personal best (well controlled)
- Yellow Zone: 50-80% of personal best (caution)
- Red Zone: <50% of personal best (medical alert)

**Action Plan:**
- Green: Continue routine medications
- Yellow: Add quick-relief inhaler, contact provider if not improving
- Red: Use quick-relief inhaler, seek immediate medical care

**Trigger Avoidance:**
- Identify and document triggers
- Common triggers: allergens, exercise, cold air, smoke, infections
- Recommend environmental controls
- Consider allergy testing if appropriate

## Emergency Protocols

### Anaphylaxis Management

**Recognition:**
- Sudden onset (minutes to hours)
- Skin: hives, itching, flushing
- Respiratory: wheezing, stridor, difficulty breathing
- Cardiovascular: low blood pressure, rapid heart rate
- GI: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, cramping

**Immediate Treatment:**
1. Call 911
2. Administer epinephrine (EpiPen) immediately
   - Adult: 0.3 mg intramuscular (outer thigh)
   - Child: 0.15 mg intramuscular
3. Position patient lying down with legs elevated
4. Maintain airway
5. Administer oxygen if available
6. Second dose of epinephrine if no improvement in 5-15 minutes
7. Transfer to emergency department (even if symptoms improve)

**Documentation:**
- Time of reaction onset
- Suspected allergen
- Symptoms
- Time of epinephrine administration
- Response to treatment
- Vital signs
- Disposition

### Chest Pain Protocol

**Immediate Assessment:**
- Onset, location, quality, radiation
- Associated symptoms: shortness of breath, nausea, sweating
- Aggravating/relieving factors
- Risk factors: age, diabetes, hypertension, smoking, family history

**Actions:**
1. If suspected cardiac: Call 911 immediately
2. Keep patient calm and still
3. Loosen tight clothing
4. Obtain vital signs
5. Administer aspirin (if not allergic) while awaiting EMS
6. Do not allow patient to drive themselves
7. Stay with patient until EMS arrives

**Documentation:**
- Time of chest pain onset
- Patient description of pain (1-10 scale)
- Vital signs
- Time 911 called
- Interventions provided
- EMS arrival and transfer

### Seizure Management

**During Seizure:**
1. Protect patient from injury
2. Clear area of hard or sharp objects
3. Cushion head
4. Turn patient on side if possible (recovery position)
5. Loosen tight clothing around neck
6. Time the seizure
7. Do NOT restrain patient
8. Do NOT put anything in patient's mouth
9. Stay with patient

**Call 911 If:**
- Seizure lasts >5 minutes
- Second seizure occurs
- Patient is injured
- Patient is pregnant
- Patient has diabetes
- Seizure occurs in water
- First-time seizure
- Patient does not return to normal consciousness

**Post-Seizure Care:**
- Keep patient on side
- Monitor airway and breathing
- Check for injuries
- Allow patient to rest
- Reorient patient gently
- Document seizure details: duration, type, post-ictal state

## Preventive Care Guidelines

### Adult Preventive Care Schedule

**Ages 18-39:**
- Annual wellness visit
- Blood pressure: Every 2 years (if normal)
- Cholesterol: Every 5 years (starting age 20)
- Diabetes screening: Every 3 years (if risk factors)
- Women: Pap smear every 3 years (21-29), Pap + HPV every 5 years (30+)
- Women: Mammogram discussion starting age 40
- Colorectal cancer screening discussion starting age 45
- Vaccinations: Tdap booster every 10 years, annual flu shot

**Ages 40-64:**
- Annual wellness visit
- Blood pressure: Annually
- Cholesterol: Every 5 years
- Diabetes screening: Every 3 years
- Women: Mammogram annually starting age 40-50 (discuss risks/benefits)
- Colorectal cancer screening: Age 45-75 (colonoscopy, FIT, or other approved method)
- Vaccinations: Tdap, annual flu, COVID-19 boosters
- Shingles vaccine: Age 50+

**Ages 65+:**
- Annual wellness visit (Medicare Annual Wellness Visit)
- Blood pressure: Every visit
- Fall risk assessment
- Cognitive screening
- Depression screening
- Bone density scan (women)
- Vaccinations: Pneumococcal, shingles, annual flu, COVID-19
- Discuss advanced directives

### Pediatric Well-Child Visit Schedule

**First Year:**
- Newborn visit (3-5 days)
- 1 month, 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, 9 months, 12 months

**Second Year:**
- 15 months, 18 months, 24 months

**Age 2-21:**
- Annual well-child visits

**Components of Well-Child Visit:**
- Growth assessment (height, weight, head circumference)
- Developmental screening
- Physical examination
- Age-appropriate immunizations
- Vision and hearing screening
- Anticipatory guidance (safety, nutrition, development)
- Parent education and support

## Quality Metrics and Documentation

### Clinical Documentation Standards

**SOAP Note Format:**
- **S**ubjective: Patient's complaint in their own words
- **O**bjective: Measurable findings (vital signs, exam, labs)
- **A**ssessment: Diagnosis or clinical impression
- **P**lan: Treatment, medications, follow-up, patient education

**Required Elements:**
- Date and time of encounter
- Chief complaint
- History of present illness
- Relevant past medical history
- Current medications and allergies
- Physical exam findings
- Assessment and diagnosis codes
- Treatment plan and instructions
- Patient education provided
- Follow-up plan
- Physician signature and credentials

### Performance Measures

**Patient Safety:**
- Medication error rate: <1%
- Hospital readmission rate: <15%
- Healthcare-associated infection rate: 0%
- Adverse event reporting: 100%

**Clinical Quality:**
- Diabetes HbA1c control (<8%): >70% of patients
- Hypertension control (<140/90): >60% of patients
- Colorectal cancer screening: >75% of eligible patients
- Breast cancer screening: >80% of eligible women
- Immunization rates: >90% of age-appropriate vaccines

**Patient Experience:**
- Patient satisfaction score: >4.5/5
- Wait time <15 minutes: >80% of appointments
- Same-day appointment availability: >70%
- Call-back time <24 hours: 100%
