# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## For Patients

### Account and Access

**Q: How do I create a patient account?**
A: Click "Sign Up" on the login page, fill in your personal information (name, email, phone, date of birth), create a password, and verify your email address. Once verified, you can log in and complete your medical profile.

**Q: I forgot my password. How do I reset it?**
A: Click "Forgot Password" on the login page, enter your registered email address, and follow the instructions sent to your email. The reset link expires after 24 hours. If you don't receive the email, check your spam folder.

**Q: Can I access my account from my phone?**
A: Yes! Our patient portal is mobile-responsive and works on all smartphones and tablets. Simply visit the website from your mobile browser and log in with your credentials. We recommend bookmarking the page for quick access.

**Q: How do I update my personal information?**
A: Log into your patient portal, go to "Profile Settings," and update your information such as address, phone number, emergency contact, or insurance details. Always keep this information current for better care coordination.

### Appointments

**Q: How far in advance can I book an appointment?**
A: You can schedule appointments up to 3 months in advance. For routine care, we recommend booking 1-2 weeks ahead. Urgent appointments may be available within 24-48 hours depending on doctor availability.

**Q: Can I choose a specific doctor?**
A: Yes! When booking an appointment, you can select your preferred doctor from the list of available providers. If you don't have a preference, you can choose "Any Available Doctor" for earlier appointment times.

**Q: What if I need to cancel or reschedule?**
A: You can cancel or reschedule up to 24 hours before your appointment through your patient portal without any fee. Cancellations with less than 12 hours notice may incur a $25 cancellation fee. For same-day changes, please call the clinic directly.

**Q: What happens if I'm running late?**
A: If you're running more than 10 minutes late, please call the clinic immediately. We'll do our best to accommodate you, but appointments may need to be shortened or rescheduled to avoid delays for other patients.

**Q: Do you offer virtual/telehealth appointments?**
A: Yes, we offer virtual appointments for many consultation types including follow-ups, medication reviews, and non-emergency consultations. Select "Virtual Appointment" when booking. You'll receive a video link before your appointment.

**Q: How do I know my appointment is confirmed?**
A: You'll receive a confirmation email immediately after booking. We also send reminder notifications 24 hours before (email) and 2 hours before (SMS if enabled). Check your patient portal to see all confirmed appointments.

### Medical Records and Documents

**Q: How do I access my medical records?**
A: Log into your patient portal and click "Medical Records." You can view consultation notes, lab results, imaging reports, prescriptions, and vaccination records. Most records are available within 48 hours of your appointment.

**Q: Can I download or print my medical records?**
A: Yes! You can download any document from your medical records section as a PDF. This is useful for keeping personal copies or sharing with other healthcare providers.

**Q: How do I request my records be sent to another doctor?**
A: Go to "Medical Records" in your portal and click "Request Transfer." Complete the authorization form with the receiving provider's information. Electronic transfers typically take 3-5 business days.

**Q: When will my test results be available?**
A: Most lab results are posted to your portal within 3-5 business days. Imaging results may take 5-7 days. You'll receive a notification when results are available. Abnormal results will be discussed via phone call from your doctor.

**Q: Can family members access my medical records?**
A: Only with your written authorization. You can grant access through the "Share Access" feature in your portal. You can specify what information can be shared and for how long. Access can be revoked at any time.

### Prescriptions and Medications

**Q: How do I get a prescription refill?**
A: Log into your portal, go to "Prescriptions," select the medication needing refill, and click "Request Refill." Your doctor will review the request within 24 hours. Some medications require an office visit for refill authorization.

**Q: Where will my prescription be sent?**
A: Prescriptions are sent electronically to your preferred pharmacy listed in your profile. You can update your pharmacy information anytime in "Profile Settings."

**Q: How long does a prescription refill request take?**
A: Most refill requests are processed within 24 hours during business days. Please request refills at least 72 hours before you run out of medication.

**Q: Can I get prescriptions without an appointment?**
A: For existing medications, your doctor may approve refills without an appointment if you've been seen recently. New prescriptions or significant medication changes always require an appointment.

### Billing and Insurance

**Q: What payment methods do you accept?**
A: We accept cash, credit/debit cards (Visa, MasterCard, American Express), and most major insurance plans. Insurance co-pays are collected at the time of service.

**Q: When will I receive my bill?**
A: Invoices are generated after your appointment and insurance claims are processed (typically 2-3 weeks). You'll receive an email notification when your bill is ready in your patient portal.

**Q: Can I set up a payment plan?**
A: Yes, payment plans are available for bills over $500. Contact our billing department at billing@multiclinic.com or call to set up an interest-free plan. Minimum monthly payment is $100.

**Q: How do I update my insurance information?**
A: Update your insurance details in "Profile Settings" under "Insurance Information." Please update this before each appointment to ensure proper billing and coverage verification.

**Q: Do you accept my insurance?**
A: We accept most major insurance providers. Contact our billing department or check your insurance provider's website to verify we're in-network. Out-of-network services may have different coverage.

**Q: Why am I being charged if I have insurance?**
A: Your insurance may require co-pays, deductibles, or coinsurance. The bill shows your portion after insurance processing. Contact your insurance provider if you have questions about your coverage.

### Communication

**Q: How do I message my doctor?**
A: Use the secure messaging feature in your patient portal. Go to "Messages" and select your doctor. Use this for non-urgent questions and follow-ups. Expect a response within 24-48 business hours.

**Q: What do I do in a medical emergency?**
A: Call 911 for life-threatening emergencies. Do NOT use the patient portal messaging for emergencies. For urgent but non-life-threatening issues, call our 24/7 nurse line at 1-800-NURSE-24.

**Q: How will I receive appointment reminders?**
A: By default, you'll receive email reminders 24 hours before and SMS reminders 2 hours before appointments. You can customize your notification preferences in "Profile Settings."

## For Doctors

### System and Workflow

**Q: How do I access my schedule?**
A: Log into the system and your daily schedule appears on the dashboard. Click "My Schedule" to view appointments for different dates, see patient information, and manage your calendar.

**Q: Can I block time off in my schedule?**
A: Yes, go to "My Availability" to set regular working hours and block specific dates for time off. Submit time-off requests at least 2 weeks in advance through the system for approval by the clinic administrator.

**Q: How do I update my availability for appointments?**
A: Access "My Availability" settings to adjust your working hours, appointment types you offer, and consultation duration. Changes take effect within 24 hours. Notify the clinic administrator of permanent changes.

**Q: Where do I document patient encounters?**
A: Click on the patient's appointment, then "Create Note." Use the SOAP note template. All notes must be completed within 24 hours of the appointment. Save drafts are available if you need to complete documentation later.

### Patient Care

**Q: How do I view a patient's medical history?**
A: Click on the patient's name from your schedule or search in "Patient Records." You'll see their complete medical history, previous visits, lab results, current medications, and allergies.

**Q: How do I send a prescription?**
A: During or after an appointment, go to "Prescriptions" in the patient's chart. Enter medication details, dosage, duration, and refills. Select patient's preferred pharmacy. The e-prescription is sent electronically.

**Q: How do I approve prescription refill requests?**
A: Refill requests appear in your "Tasks" or "Messages." Review the patient's chart and either approve the refill or schedule an appointment if evaluation is needed. Respond within 24 hours.

**Q: How do I order lab tests or imaging?**
A: In the patient's chart, go to "Orders" and select the appropriate tests. Include clinical indication and urgency. Orders are sent electronically to the lab/imaging center. Results post automatically to the patient's chart.

**Q: How do I refer a patient to a specialist?**
A: Create a referral in the patient's chart under "Referrals." Include diagnosis, reason for referral, and any relevant test results. The referral is sent to the specialist and tracked in the system.

### Results and Follow-up

**Q: How do I review lab results?**
A: Lab results appear in "Pending Results" on your dashboard. Results are also filed in each patient's chart. Review and acknowledge all results, especially abnormal findings, within 24-48 hours.

**Q: How do I communicate abnormal results to patients?**
A: For abnormal results, call the patient directly. Document the conversation in their chart including date, time, results discussed, and plan. Normal results can be released through the portal with a brief note.

**Q: How do I schedule follow-up appointments for patients?**
A: During the appointment, use "Schedule Follow-up" to book the next appointment before the patient leaves. For later follow-ups, send instructions via secure message or have reception schedule.

## For Receptionists

### Patient Management

**Q: How do I register a new patient?**
A: Click "New Patient Registration," enter all required information (name, DOB, contact, insurance), scan their ID and insurance card, and create their account. Send them portal access instructions via email.

**Q: What if a patient doesn't have their insurance card?**
A: Collect as much insurance information as they can provide (carrier name, member ID). Mark the account for insurance verification and follow up within 24 hours. Patient may be responsible for payment if insurance can't be verified.

**Q: How do I check a patient in?**
A: Search for the patient's appointment, verify their identity and appointment details, update any changed information, collect co-pay, mark them as "Checked In," and notify the medical staff.

**Q: What do I do if a patient is late?**
A: If less than 10 minutes late, check them in normally. If 10-15 minutes late, check with the doctor about accommodating them. If more than 15 minutes late, the appointment may need to be rescheduled.

### Scheduling

**Q: How do I schedule an appointment?**
A: Click "New Appointment," select patient (or create new patient), choose doctor and appointment type, select available time slot, add any notes, and confirm. Patient receives automatic confirmation email.

**Q: What if no appointment slots are available?**
A: Check other providers' schedules, add patient to waitlist for cancellations, or schedule at another clinic location. For urgent cases, consult with clinic administrator about adding a same-day slot.

**Q: How do I handle appointment cancellations?**
A: Find the appointment, click "Cancel," select cancellation reason, check if cancellation fee applies, notify the patient, and offer to reschedule. Update the waitlist if applicable.

**Q: Can I override the scheduling system?**
A: Only clinic administrators can override scheduling restrictions. For special cases (emergencies, VIP patients, provider requests), contact your supervisor for approval.

### Phone and Communication

**Q: How should I answer incoming calls?**
A: Answer within 3 rings: "Good [morning/afternoon], [Clinic Name], this is [Your Name], how may I help you?" Be professional, courteous, and helpful. Document important call details.

**Q: How do I transfer calls to the right person?**
A: Appointments → handle directly; Medical questions → nurse line; Billing → billing dept; Prescriptions → nurse line; Emergencies → assess and direct appropriately or call 911.

**Q: What if a caller has a medical emergency?**
A: If life-threatening, tell them to call 911 or go to the ER immediately. For urgent but not emergent issues, transfer to the on-call nurse. Always document emergency calls.

## For Clinic Administrators

### Staff Management

**Q: How do I add a new staff member to the system?**
A: Go to "Staff Management," click "Add New Staff," enter their information, assign their role (doctor, receptionist, nurse), set permissions, and create login credentials. Provide training before granting full access.

**Q: How do I manage doctor schedules?**
A: Access "Doctor Management" to view and edit each doctor's availability, working hours, and time-off requests. Approve or deny time-off requests and ensure adequate coverage for all shifts.

**Q: How do I remove a staff member who left?**
A: Go to their profile and select "Deactivate Account." This preserves their historical records but prevents system access. Do not delete accounts as this affects historical data and reporting.

### Operations

**Q: How do I generate reports?**
A: Go to "Reports" section and select report type (appointments, revenue, patient volume, etc.). Set date range and filters, then generate. Reports can be downloaded as PDF or Excel.

**Q: How do I monitor clinic performance?**
A: The admin dashboard shows key metrics: daily appointments, revenue, patient satisfaction scores, wait times, and no-show rates. Use these to identify trends and areas for improvement.

**Q: How do I handle patient complaints?**
A: Document all complaints in "Quality Assurance" section. Investigate thoroughly, determine resolution, follow up with patient within 48 hours, and implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence.

**Q: How do I update clinic settings?**
A: Access "Clinic Settings" to modify working hours, appointment types, fees, notification templates, and system preferences. Major changes should be communicated to all staff before implementation.

### Billing and Finance

**Q: How do I track clinic revenue?**
A: Use the Financial Reports section to view daily, weekly, and monthly revenue. Reports show total revenue, outstanding payments, insurance claims status, and payment method breakdown.

**Q: How do I handle billing disputes?**
A: Review the disputed invoice and patient's concern. Check insurance EOB and payment records. Resolve directly with patient when possible or escalate to billing department for complex issues.

## Technical Support

**Q: What browsers are supported?**
A: We support the latest versions of Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, and Microsoft Edge. For best performance, keep your browser updated.

**Q: What if the system is running slowly?**
A: Clear your browser cache, close unnecessary tabs, check your internet connection, or try a different browser. Contact IT support if issues persist.

**Q: I can't log in. What should I do?**
A: Verify you're using the correct email and password. Try resetting your password. Clear browser cache or try incognito mode. If still unable to access, contact IT support immediately.

**Q: How do I report a system error?**
A: Document the error (screenshot if possible), note what you were doing when it occurred, and report to IT support via email (itsupport@multiclinic.com) or phone. Include your name, role, and contact information.

**Q: Is my data backed up?**
A: Yes, all system data is automatically backed up every hour. Daily full backups are stored securely off-site. In case of data loss, recovery is typically completed within 4 hours.

**Q: How secure is patient information?**
A: All patient data is encrypted in transit and at rest. The system is HIPAA-compliant with regular security audits. Access is logged and monitored. Multi-factor authentication is available for enhanced security.

## General Questions

**Q: What are your clinic hours?**
A: Standard hours are Monday-Friday 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM, Saturday 9:00 AM - 2:00 PM. Individual clinic locations may have different hours. Check your specific clinic's schedule in the system or website.

**Q: Do you offer after-hours care?**
A: For urgent medical questions outside business hours, call our 24/7 nurse line at 1-800-NURSE-24. For emergencies, always call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.

**Q: How do I provide feedback about my experience?**
A: We value your input! Complete the satisfaction survey emailed after your appointment, leave a review in your patient portal, or contact us directly through the feedback form. Your feedback helps us improve.

**Q: Are my medical records private?**
A: Absolutely. We are fully HIPAA-compliant. Your medical information is confidential and only shared with your consent or as required by law. All staff are trained in privacy protection.

**Q: Can I get a copy of my medical records?**
A: Yes, you have the right to access your medical records. Download them directly from your patient portal or submit a formal request for paper copies (processing fee may apply).

**Q: How do I file a complaint?**
A: Contact our Patient Relations department at patientrelations@multiclinic.com or call during business hours. Complaints are taken seriously, investigated thoroughly, and resolved promptly with follow-up communication.
